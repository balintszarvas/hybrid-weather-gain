\abstract{\addtocontents{toc}{\vspace{1em}} % Add a gap in the Contents, for aesthetics
This thesis presents a fully differentiable hybrid weather modeling framework that couples a spectral dynamical core with a Graph Neural Network (GNN) neural closure. While traditional numerical atmospheric models are computationally expensive and purely data-driven models often suffer from physical inconsistencies and long-term error accumulation, this hybrid approach successfully balances structural physical priors with data-driven flexibility.

Trained end-to-end using an a posteriori trajectory fitting approach, the hybrid model outperforms both purely numerical and purely machine-learning baselines. To interpret the behavior of the neural closure, the author introduces a novel "closure gain" that projects the GNN's Jacobian onto an orthogonal basis. This cross-scale analysis reveals the closure's dynamic role: it actively amplifies tendencies at small, heavily filtered spatial scales to restore missing physical variability, while simultaneously acting as a diffusive stabilizer at larger scales and extended lead times to prevent chaotic error growth. Ultimately, the research demonstrates that interpretable hybrid models can deliver competitive weather forecasts while yielding actionable, mechanistic insights into the internal dynamics of learned physical corrections.
}
